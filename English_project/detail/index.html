Hehuan Mountain Main Peak Trail

The main peak of Hehuan is one of the Taiwan Highest peak. There are no mountains around it that block the view. The vision is extremely wide.
Standing on the top of the mountain and looking around, you can have a panoramic view of Hehuan Peak, Qilai Mountain, Nenggao Mountain, Nanhu Mountain, and Central Jianshan Mountain.
It is a relatively simple route among the Baiyue routes in Taiwan, with a total length of 1.8 kilometers. The road is easy to walk and the ridges are clear. Beginners can also challenge it.
It takes about one hour to reach the triangle point of the main peak of Hehuan.
Go to Hehuan Mountain Main Peak Trail ->

Hehuan Chien Peak

One of the Hehuan peaks, it is famous for its pyramid-like shape.
The hinterland at the top of the mountain is small but the outlook is good.
You can have a panoramic view of the Hehuan peaks and the Hehuan Mountain circle valley, and you can also overlook the Qilaizhu, Bei and other central mountain ranges.
The whole trail is 400 meters and takes about 30 minutes.
Although the trail is not long, the mountain rises from the ground, the slope is steep, and the difficulty is relatively high.
Go to Hehuan Chien Peak ->

Hehuan North Peak

One of Taiwan highest peak, the highest peak in the Hehuan Mountains, and one of the top ten peaks. The continuous arrow bamboo grass slopes along the way are like the grasslands of the Alps reaching to the sky. On the ridgeline close to the top of the mountain, a huge reflective plate was erected, which is the most obvious symbol of Hehuan North Peak. After that, the field of vision became wider and wider, and sometimes you can enjoy the sea of clouds rolling in the valley. The top of the mountain has an excellent outlook, and you can have a panoramic view of the distant and near mountains such as Yushan, Snow Mountain, Hehuan Peak, Qilai and Nanhu. The total length is two kilometers, and it takes 3 hours to go back and forth. The road surface is relatively primitive and difficult to climb, and the difficulty is relatively high.

Bong Bong Train

Taipingshan used to be a forest farm and the indispensable equipment, such as cableway, cabin, forest railway and steam-skidder etc., are still well preserved. Among them, Bong Bong Train played an important role in transporting timber between different locations in the early days. One of the most popular tourist attractions of ecotourism in Taipingshan, the Maosing Line was restored in 1985 and re-operated in 1991. The Bong Bong train station is on the left side of central stairway at Taipingshan Villa. The Maosing Line is 3 km long and it takes 20 minutes to arrive at the terminal- Maosing Station.
Go to Bong Bong Train ->

Price:$180/per(ticket)

Su'ao Cold Springs

The Su'ao cold springs have a temperature of less than 22°C and the water from these springs are clear, colorless and emit no smell. This rare type of cold springs is only found at a few places around the world. As the water temperature of the cold springs is lower than our body temperature, you will first feel cold when immersing into the water, however, after having soaked for some 5 minutes, your body will start to warm up. As the water of these cold springs contains high levels of carbon dioxide, it can also be used for food and beverages, such as yokan and naturally carbonated soda.
Go to Su'ao Cold Springs ->

Price:$120/per

Jiaoxi Hot Spring

Jiaoxi Hot Spring is one of a very rare hot springs in Taiwan that is extremely rich in minerals. The water of Jiaoxi Hot Spring is a sodium bicarbonate hot spring that is extremely rich in sodium, magnesium, calcium, potassium, and carbonic acid ions. Being sodium bicarbonate hot springs, Jiaoxi Hot Spring are odorless and clear, and it maintains a temperature of around 58° C. A soak in the pool is relaxing and comfortable, leaving the skin smooth but not sticky. As the water is drinkable, Jiaoxi Hot Springs vegetable is another well-known feature of Jiaosi.
Go to Jiaoxi Hot Spring ->
